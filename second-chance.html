8 Job Search Tactics That Actually Work

These are the tactics that separate people who get hired from people who keep applying. A skills-first resume from ResumeBuzz applies several of these automatically.

01
Pre-screen the employer
Call HR and ask their fair-chance policy before investing time in an application. Saves hours of applying to places that will disqualify you automatically.
02
Target Ban-the-Box states first
37+ states delay the criminal history question until after you prove your skills. Your application competes on merit first. Google your state's specific law.
03
Run your own background check
Errors and outdated records are common. Dispute them before applying. Use AnnualCreditReport.com and your state's court records system.
04
Lead with skills, not gaps
Skills-first resume format puts your value front and center. Address the record honestly only when directly asked. Never volunteer it in the resume.
05
Get a quick certification
Food handler, OSHA 10, forklift, or CDL permit can all be earned fast and signal readiness. Certifications close gaps and show initiative.
06
Apply through reentry orgs
Their employer relationships convert at much higher rates than cold applications. The employer already agreed to evaluate fairly before you walk in.
07
Network back through old contacts
Referral hires bypass the resume screen entirely. Even a casual acquaintance who vouches for you changes the evaluation dynamic completely.
08
Use Google operators
Search "hiring felons" + your city, or "second chance" + your industry. You will find employers who have publicly stated their policy and aren't on the big boards.

Know Your Rights

Legal Landscape

Understanding these laws helps you push back on unlawful screening and target jurisdictions where your application gets the fairest shot.

Ban the Box / Fair Chance Laws
As of 2026, 37+ states and 150+ cities have Ban the Box or Fair Chance laws. These laws delay criminal history questions until after a conditional offer, require individualized assessment of conviction relevance, and may limit reporting periods (often 7 years in CA, KS, MA). Look up your state's specific law before applying.
Federal Fair Chance Act
Delays criminal inquiry for federal hiring and federal contractors until after a conditional offer. If a federal employer or contractor asks about your record before making an offer, they are breaking federal law. Report violations to your state's EEOC office.
EEOC Green Factors
Employers must consider three factors before rejecting you for a criminal record: (1) nature of the offense, (2) time elapsed since the conviction, and (3) relevance of the offense to the specific job duties. Blanket bans on all convictions are not legal under EEOC guidance.
Work Opportunity Tax Credit
Employers who hire you may receive up to $9,600 in federal tax credits. Use this as leverage in negotiations. Mention it directly: "I qualify for WOTC, which means you may receive a tax credit of up to $9,600 for hiring me." This shifts the conversation.
