8 Job Search Tactics That Actually Work

These separate people who get hired from people who keep applying. Work smarter, not just harder. The hive rewards both.

01
Pre-screen the employer
Call HR and ask their fair-chance policy before investing time in an application.
02
Target Ban-the-Box states first
37+ states delay the criminal history question until after you prove your skills.
03
Run your own background check
Errors are common. Dispute them before applying using your state's court records.
04
Lead with skills, not gaps
Skills-first format puts your value front. Address the record only when directly asked.
05
Get a quick certification
OSHA 10, forklift, food handler, or CDL permit signal readiness and close gaps fast.
06
Apply through reentry orgs
Their employer relationships convert at much higher rates than cold applications.
07
Network back through old contacts
Referral hires bypass the resume screen entirely. Even a casual voucher changes everything.
08
Use Google operators
Search "hiring felons" + your city or "second chance" + your industry to find quiet employers.

Know Your Rights

Legal Landscape

Understanding these laws helps you push back on unlawful screening and target the states where your record carries the least sting.

Ban the Box / Fair Chance Laws ↗
As of 2026, 37+ states and 150+ cities have Ban the Box or Fair Chance laws. These laws delay criminal history questions until after a conditional offer, require individualized assessment of conviction relevance, and may limit reporting periods (often 7 years in CA, KS, MA). Look up your state's specific law before applying.
Federal Fair Chance Act ↗
Delays criminal inquiry for federal hiring and federal contractors until after a conditional offer. If a federal employer or contractor asks about your record before making an offer, they are breaking federal law. Report violations to your state's EEOC office.
EEOC Green Factors ↗
Employers must consider three factors before rejecting you for a criminal record: (1) nature of the offense, (2) time elapsed since the conviction, and (3) relevance of the offense to the specific job duties. Blanket bans on all convictions are not legal under EEOC guidance.
Work Opportunity Tax Credit ↗
Employers who hire you may receive up to $9,600 in federal tax credits. Use this as leverage in negotiations. Mention it directly: "I qualify for WOTC, which means you may receive a tax credit of up to $9,600 for hiring me." This shifts the conversation.
